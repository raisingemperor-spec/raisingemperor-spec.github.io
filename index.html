3. Monitoring Body Mass Index (BMI) for Longevity

Physical health is your most valuable asset. The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely accepted screening tool used by medical professionals to determine if a person is in a healthy weight range for their height. While it doesn't measure body fat directly, it correlates strongly with metabolic and disease risks. Maintaining a "Normal" BMI (between 18.5 and 24.9) is associated with reduced risks of heart disease, diabetes, and other chronic conditions.
